The following year I gave my site a major redesign. I installed a text editor to streamline writing and kept refining details. I was still learning. I read a lot, experimented with speed and security, and aimed to make my site stand out.

But over time it became exhausting. I had to choose whether to focus on writing or continue maintaining the site from scratch.

üåÄ Discovering WordPress

During this period I kept hearing about WordPress. It was becoming a standard and people were praising its features. When I found out a group of local developers was offering free hosting, I jumped in.

I installed WordPress immediately and chose a theme called News that suited my needs. After making minor adjustments I transferred all my posts.

Everything was smooth until my hosting provider, Ikra Hosting, decided to upgrade their servers.

Because I was using their free plan, they deleted everything. No backups. No replies to my emails.

üí° Why Reliable Hosting Matters

This was an important lesson. Never work with someone just because the deal is cheap or familiar. It ends up being more expensive in the long run.

Agora Hosting is not cheap but they are responsive and reliable. I once asked them to fix a typo in my domain name and redirect traffic from the old one to the new one. They did it quickly. My site was on a Windows server and I requested a move to Linux. They handled it without hesitation.

üìò Final Thoughts

This journey has been more than a technical exercise. It has been about ownership, growth and learning from mistakes. From a simple Blogger site to a customized WordPress-powered platform, the experience has taught me that taking control of your content is always worth it.
